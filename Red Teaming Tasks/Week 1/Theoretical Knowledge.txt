1. Fundamentals Red Teaming
What to Learn:

Core Concepts: Purpose of Red Teaming (simulating adversaries to test defenses), attack lifecycle (reconnaissance, exploitation, persistence), MITRE ATT&CK framework.
Key Objectives: Understand Red Teaming’s role in improving security through offensive techniques.
How to Learn:

Watch YouTube tutorials (e.g., TryHackMe Red Team Fundamentals).
Complete interactive quizzes on Cybrary (free tier, search for Red Team basics).


2. Reconnaissance and Information Gathering
What to Learn:

Reconnaissance Types: Passive (OSINT, WHOIS) and active (port scanning, service enumeration).
Tools: Nmap (network scanning), Maltego (OSINT), theHarvester (email enumeration).
How to Learn:

Study OSINT techniques using OSINT Framework.
Practice active scanning with Nmap in a lab environment.


3. Exploitation and Vulnerability Assessment
What to Learn:

Exploitation Basics: Exploiting vulnerabilities, privilege escalation, payload delivery.
Tools: Metasploit (exploitation framework), OpenVAS (vulnerability scanner).
How to Learn:

Follow Metasploit Unleashed (free course).
Use OpenVAS to scan for vulnerabilities.


4. Post-Exploitation and Persistence
What to Learn:

Post-Exploitation: Data exfiltration, lateral movement, maintaining access.
Tools: Mimikatz (credential dumping), Netcat (backdoors), PowerShell (scripting).
How to Learn:

Study post-exploitation with TryHackMe’s Red Team Track (free rooms available).
Practice persistence techniques in a controlled lab.


5. Reporting and Red Team Operations
What to Learn:

Reporting: Documenting findings, attack paths, and recommendations.
Red Team Operations: Planning, scoping, and rules of engagement (RoE).
How to Learn:

Use SANS Red Team report templates.
Study RoE from Red Team Field Manual excerpts online.
